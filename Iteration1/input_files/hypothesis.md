The current model's performance plateau at $\ell > 5000$ and the smoothing effect of the wMSE objective can be overcome by implementing a Multi-Scale Patch-GAN discriminator that operates in the wavelet domain. Instead of a standard pixel-wise adversarial loss, the discriminator will evaluate the consistency of the reconstructed tSZ signal across multiple wavelet decomposition levels (e.g., using Stationary Wavelet Transform). This approach forces the model to recover high-frequency textures and non-Gaussian cluster morphologies—which are currently suppressed by the wMSE—by penalizing the loss of power in specific frequency bands where baryonic feedback signatures reside. By conditioning the discriminator on the CIB-derived spatial priors, the model will learn to distinguish between physical small-scale gas fluctuations and noise-induced artifacts, effectively acting as a learned, non-linear deconvolution filter that preserves the phase coherence of the tSZ signal at small scales without the hallucination risks associated with global spectral constraints.
