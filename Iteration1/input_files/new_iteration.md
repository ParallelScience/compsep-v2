- Decision: ITERATE
- Justification: The current project has reached a critical "diagnostic maturity." The previous failure was not due to a lack of potential in the architecture, but rather a well-understood optimization pathology (global loss collapse on sparse data). The proposed changes—specifically the transition to cILC pre-processing, spectral difference inputs, and focal/weighted loss functions—directly address the "Publishability Delta" by moving the model from a non-functional state to a physically grounded, high-fidelity reconstruction tool. The ROI of effort is high because the infrastructure (data pipeline, U-Net architecture, and evaluation suite) is already built; the remaining work is a targeted refinement of the training objective and input representation. This iteration is essential to transform a "failed experiment" into a robust scientific contribution that demonstrates the limits and capabilities of deep learning in CMB component separation.
