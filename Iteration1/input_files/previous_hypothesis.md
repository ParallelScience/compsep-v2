The failure of the previous spectral loss suggests that global power spectrum constraints are too rigid for local patch-based reconstruction, leading to hallucination. I hypothesize that a "Spectral-Spatial Feature Alignment" (SSFA) approach, which replaces the global spectral loss with a local, multi-scale feature-matching loss in the latent space, will improve reconstruction fidelity without inducing non-physical artifacts. 

Specifically, I propose:
1. **Latent Feature Matching:** Instead of penalizing the output power spectrum, we will use a pre-trained, frozen "Perceptual Discriminator" (trained on the FLAMINGO simulation to distinguish between ground-truth tSZ and noise-corrupted maps) to provide a feature-matching loss. This forces the U-Net to match the statistical texture of the tSZ signal at multiple feature-map depths rather than enforcing a global power constraint.
2. **Phase-Coherence Training:** To address the reported 0.0 cross-correlation ($r_\ell$), we will introduce a complex-valued Fourier-space loss term that explicitly penalizes the phase difference between the predicted and ground-truth tSZ maps in the 500 < $\ell$ < 3000 range. This will enforce spatial alignment (phase coherence) without requiring the model to match the exact power amplitude at every scale, preventing the "hallucination" observed in the previous iteration.
3. **Dynamic Noise Conditioning:** We will replace static noise injection with a "Noise-Aware Normalization" layer, where the U-Net's batch normalization parameters are conditioned on the local variance of the input noise maps. This allows the model to adapt its denoising strength dynamically based on the specific SO/Planck noise realization per patch, improving performance in high-noise regimes.
