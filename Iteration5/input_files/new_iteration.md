- Decision: ITERATE
- Justification: The current project is at a critical juncture where the technical performance (SR-DAE/CDM) is strong, but the scientific validation is currently vulnerable to peer-review criticism regarding circularity and potential foreground leakage. 

1. **Structural Robustness**: The critique regarding the use of "peak tSZ" as a mass proxy is valid; using the ground-truth halo masses from the FLAMINGO catalog is a low-effort, high-impact change that transforms the validation from "self-consistent" to "scientifically rigorous."
2. **Reviewer Confidence**: Addressing the "MSE vs. Perceptual Quality" fallacy and explicitly quantifying CIB-to-tSZ leakage are essential to move the manuscript from a "technical demonstration" to a "cosmological tool." Reviewers in this field are highly sensitive to foreground leakage; providing the residual-CIB correlation coefficient is the standard of proof required for publication in top-tier journals.
3. **Opportunity Cost**: The infrastructure for these tests (halo catalogs, residual analysis) is already largely available within the simulation framework. The effort required to re-run the validation metrics is minimal compared to the potential gain in credibility. By refining the validation rather than starting a new project, we solidify the "So What?" factor, proving that the model is a reliable instrument for baryonic feedback studies rather than just a sophisticated image processor.
