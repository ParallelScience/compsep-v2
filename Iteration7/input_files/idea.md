**Title**: Spectral-Spatial Super-Resolution Denoising for Baryonic Pressure Mapping

**Description**: This objective implements a Super-Resolution Denoising Autoencoder (SR-DAE) to reconstruct 1-arcmin resolution tSZ Compton-y maps from SO LAT-band observations (90, 150, 217 GHz), utilizing high-frequency CIB maps (353–857 GHz) as auxiliary features to guide the recovery of small-scale gas pressure fluctuations. The architecture employs a multi-scale U-Net with gated cross-attention, enabling the model to learn the spatial correlation between CIB-traced star-forming regions and ionized gas flows without assuming deterministic coupling. To ensure physical validity, the training objective minimizes a composite loss function: a pixel-wise L1-reconstruction loss for the tSZ ground truth and a spectral consistency term calculated via `utils.powers` that penalizes deviations from the FLAMINGO-simulated tSZ power spectrum. By conditioning on per-patch SO and Planck noise realizations, the SR-DAE effectively disentangles the non-Gaussian tSZ signal from the Gaussian CMB background and instrument noise. This approach specifically targets the 1–5 arcmin scale, where baryonic feedback signatures are most prominent, while preventing the hallucination of features by constraining the latent space representation to the known physical power distribution of the simulation.
