The current Conditional Diffusion Model (CDM) successfully captures the stochastic nature of tSZ signals but remains limited by the "simulation-dependency" of its learned priors, specifically regarding the sub-grid AGN feedback prescriptions in FLAMINGO. I hypothesize that by implementing a **Physics-Informed Contrastive Learning (PICL) pre-training stage**, we can decouple the model's structural priors from the specific FLAMINGO feedback physics. 

Specifically, we will train an encoder to maximize the mutual information between the CIB-conditioned tSZ latent representation and the physical properties of the gas (e.g., pressure-weighted temperature and density profiles derived from the simulation) across different spatial scales. By forcing the latent space to represent these physical quantities rather than just the pixel-wise tSZ signal, the model will learn a more robust, "physics-aware" representation. This representation will then serve as the conditioning backbone for the existing CDM. I expect this to reduce the systematic bias in high-intensity cluster cores and improve the model's generalization capability when encountering diverse baryonic feedback signatures, as the model will be conditioned on the underlying physical state of the gas rather than just the simulation-specific tSZ realization.
