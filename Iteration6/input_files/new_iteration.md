- Decision: ITERATE
- Justification: The current project is at a critical juncture where the "deterministic trap" of the SR-DAE is well-identified, and the path to a high-impact publication is clear. The current results already demonstrate that the SR-DAE provides a superior "mean-field" estimate compared to linear baselines, but the scientific value is currently capped by the focus on pixel-wise metrics that are fundamentally limited by the noise floor. 

Iterating is the correct choice because:
1. **Structural Robustness**: The transition to a Conditional Diffusion Model (CDM) directly addresses the "blurriness" caused by the L1 loss, moving the project from a simple denoising task to a generative framework capable of recovering the non-Gaussian statistics required for cosmological inference.
2. **Scientific Impact**: Shifting the narrative from "pixel-perfect reconstruction" (which is physically impossible at high $\ell$) to "statistical recovery of the $Y_{SZ}-M$ relation" aligns the paper with the actual needs of the cosmology community. This moves the contribution from a technical exercise to a scientifically significant tool for baryonic feedback studies.
3. **Publishability Delta**: The current results are "stable enough" to prove the concept, but the addition of the CDM and the refinement of the loss function/validation metrics (PDF of $Y_{SZ}$, conditional scatter plots) will elevate the manuscript from a technical report to a high-tier journal submission. The ROI of effort is high because the SR-DAE backbone is already trained, making the CDM implementation a natural and high-value extension rather than a "fresh idea" pivot.
