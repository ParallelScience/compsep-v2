The observed underestimation of peak tSZ intensities in high-density regions (cores of massive galaxy clusters) is directly attributable to the dataset's inherent constraints and the nature of the provided ground truth. The dataset description specifies that the ground truth components (including tSZ) are provided with a 1 arcmin beam, while the stacked observed signal maps are smoothed with frequency-specific beams (e.g., 2.2 arcmin for 90 GHz). The SR-DAE's systematic underestimation of peak intensities in high-density regions is a common consequence of training on data where the input signal is significantly beam-smeared relative to the ground truth, combined with the pixel-wise regression loss mentioned in the results. The dataset's limitation in spatial resolution (beam-smearing) in the input maps, compared to the 1 arcmin ground truth, fundamentally constrains the model's ability to recover high-frequency, high-amplitude peak structures, leading to the observed negative bias in high-intensity regions.
